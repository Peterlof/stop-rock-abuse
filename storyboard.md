# STOP ROCK ABUSE — Video Storyboard (3:00)
## A Geological Mockumentary Trailer

**Aspect Ratio:** 16:9
**Tone:** Documentary parody — deadpan narration over dramatic visuals. Think Werner Herzog meets a geology textbook that's had enough.
**Narrator Voice:** Male or female, calm, authoritative, BBC-documentary serious. Never winks. The humor comes from treating absurd premises with total sincerity.

---

### SCENE 1 — THE OPENING (0:00–0:25)

**Shot 1 (0:00–0:06)**
VISUAL: Slow aerial pull-back over a vast, beautiful mountain range at sunrise. Golden light on exposed rock faces. Mist in the valleys. Breathtaking.
CAMERA: Drone shot, slow pull-back and rise.
SOUND: Gentle orchestral swell, nature documentary style.
NARRATION: "This is rock. It makes up seventy-five percent of Earth's crust."

**Shot 2 (0:06–0:12)**
VISUAL: Cut to a massive open-pit quarry. Industrial scale. Trucks like ants. Dust everywhere. Harsh midday light.
CAMERA: Wide establishing shot, static.
SOUND: Orchestra cuts abruptly. Industrial rumble fades in — drilling, crushing, truck engines.
NARRATION: "Fifty billion tons of it are extracted every year."

**Shot 3 (0:12–0:18)**
VISUAL: Montage — quick cuts: a jackhammer splitting stone, molten lava being sampled by scientists, a crusher machine pulverizing gravel, a marble slab being cut with a diamond wire saw. Each cut is 1.5 seconds.
CAMERA: Close-ups, handheld, slightly shaky.
SOUND: Rhythmic industrial percussion, each cut synced to a beat.
NARRATION: "Melted. Crushed. Dissolved. Blasted apart."

**Shot 4 (0:18–0:25)**
VISUAL: A single small stone sitting alone on a white background. Studio lit. Perfectly still.
CAMERA: Locked-off center frame, slow push-in.
SOUND: Complete silence for 2 beats, then a single low cello note.
NARRATION: "And in eleven billion years... not one of them has been asked how it feels."
TEXT ON SCREEN (fades in): "Until now."

### SCENE 2 — THE PROBLEM (0:25–0:55)

**Shot 5 (0:25–0:32)**

CAMERA: Static frame, motion graphics.
SOUND: Light, optimistic music underscore.
NARRATION: "Trees have thousands of advocacy groups. International holidays. A UN resolution."

**Shot 6 (0:32–0:38)**
VISUAL: Second stat card slides in: "1.5 MILLION WHALES" with icons of treaties, organizations, a musical note. Ocean blue background.
CAMERA: Static frame, motion graphics.
SOUND: Same optimistic underscore continues.
NARRATION: "Whales have treaties. Songs written about them. They can leave."

**Shot 7 (0:38–0:48)**
VISUAL: Third stat card — but this one is different. Dark, stark background. "75% OF EARTH'S CRUST" with zeros: "0 advocacy groups. 0 treaties. 0 holidays." Each zero stamps on screen with impact.
CAMERA: Static frame, but slight camera shake on each zero impact.
SOUND: Music drops out. Each zero lands with a deep, resonant thud.
NARRATION: "Rock makes up seventy-five percent of Earth's crust. Zero advocacy groups. Zero treaties. Zero holidays. It has been exposed to eleven billion years of abuse." (beat) "It cannot run."

**Shot 8 (0:48–0:55)**
VISUAL: Close-up of a weathered boulder in a desert landscape, wind blowing sand across its surface. Late afternoon light. Almost portrait-like — the rock as subject.
CAMERA: Slow dolly around the rock, shallow depth of field.
SOUND: Wind. Sparse, melancholic piano note.
NARRATION: "We're not saying rocks have feelings. We're saying they have a story. And it's overdue."

### SCENE 3 — THE ROCK CYCLE (0:55–1:20)

**Shot 9 (0:55–1:02)**
VISUAL: Animated diagram of the rock cycle — but styled like a dystopian flowchart. Igneous, sedimentary, metamorphic connected by arrows labeled "CRUSHED," "MELTED," "COMPRESSED." The cycle loops endlessly.
CAMERA: Slow zoom into the center of the cycle.
SOUND: Mechanical loop sound, like gears turning.
NARRATION: "The rock cycle. Nature's most efficient recycling program — and its cruelest."

**Shot 10 (1:02–1:08)**
VISUAL: Volcanic eruption — lava exploding from a vent, magma flowing. Dramatic, fiery, beautiful.
CAMERA: Wide shot with slow-motion lava splash.
SOUND: Deep volcanic rumble.
NARRATION: "Born from fire at twelve hundred degrees."

**Shot 11 (1:08–1:14)**
VISUAL: Underwater shot of sediment layers building up on an ocean floor, compressed under weight. Time-lapse feel.
CAMERA: Slow tilt upward through the layers.
SOUND: Deep pressure sound, creaking.
NARRATION: "Buried. Compressed. Cemented back together without consent."

**Shot 12 (1:14–1:20)**
VISUAL: Close-up of metamorphic rock — folded gneiss with dramatic banding patterns. Beautiful but violent-looking geology.
CAMERA: Macro lens, slow pan across the folds.
SOUND: Low tension strings.
NARRATION: "Then pressure-cooked into something new. No one asks what it wanted to be."

### SCENE 4 — HUMAN EXPLOITATION (1:20–1:50)

**Shot 13 (1:20–1:28)**
VISUAL: Rapid montage of human rock use — concrete being poured, glass being blown, a smartphone being assembled, toothpaste squeezed onto a brush, a diamond ring on a finger, a granite countertop being installed.
CAMERA: Quick cuts, 1 second each. Mixed close-ups and medium shots.
SOUND: Upbeat, almost commercial-like music — like a tech product ad.
NARRATION: "Your phone. Your toothpaste. Your countertop. Your glass. Your roads, your buildings, your jewelry. All rock. Processed, repackaged, and sold back to you without a single credit."

**Shot 14 (1:28–1:36)**
VISUAL: Split screen — LEFT: a beautiful marble statue (Michelangelo's David). RIGHT: a quarry with a massive marble block being cut from the hillside, scar visible.
CAMERA: Static split screen, both images slowly zooming.
SOUND: Music shifts to something more somber.
NARRATION: "They call this art." (gestures to statue) "Nobody photographs what it looked like before."

**Shot 15 (1:36–1:42)**
VISUAL: A cement factory with smokestacks billowing. Overlaid animated text: "5-8% OF GLOBAL CO₂ EMISSIONS."
CAMERA: Wide industrial shot, smoke drifting across frame.
SOUND: Industrial drone.
NARRATION: "The cement industry alone produces five to eight percent of global carbon emissions. We heat rock to fourteen hundred and fifty degrees — to glue other rocks together."

**Shot 16 (1:42–1:50)**
VISUAL: Close-up of a concrete sidewalk. Camera tilts up to reveal it stretches to the horizon — endless.
CAMERA: Low angle, dramatic tilt up.
SOUND: A beat of silence.
NARRATION: "We crush rocks into aggregate, then glue them back together with cement. We break rocks — to build worse rocks, faster."

## CHARACTERS & RECURRING ELEMENTS

- **The Narrator**: Never seen. Calm, authoritative, completely sincere. Treats rock advocacy with the gravity of a war crimes tribunal.
- **The Lone Stone**: A single, unremarkable stone that appears in Shots 4 and 25 as a visual anchor — the everyrock.
- **Text Cards**: The testimonial quotes (Scene 5) should feel like witness statements — serif font, black background, white text, typewriter animation.
- **The Site**: Shown in its actual form (Scene 6) — the visual quality of the site itself is part of the pitch.

## MUSIC & SOUND DESIGN NOTES

- **0:00–0:18**: Nature documentary grandeur → abrupt industrial cut
- **0:18–0:55**: Sparse, melancholic — piano, cello, silence used as punctuation
- **0:55–1:20**: Mechanical, cyclical — matches the rock cycle's endless loop
- **1:20–1:50**: Starts commercial-bright, shifts to somber industrial
- **1:50–2:15**: Near silence — let the quotes breathe
- **2:15–2:45**: Modern, confident electronic — this is the "sell" section
- **2:45–3:00**: Return to silence and single cello. End on stillness.

## KEY STATS FOR ON-SCREEN GRAPHICS

- 50 billion tons of sand & gravel extracted annually
- 1.5 billion tons of stone crushed in the US alone
- 11 billion+ years of rock abuse across the universe
- 5-8% of global CO₂ from cement production
- 48.5 tons of meteoritic material burned by Earth's atmosphere daily
- 0 rocks who consented to any of this
