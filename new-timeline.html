SEASON 1: EPISODE GUIDE

S01E01

The Big Bang — The Crime Scene

Runtime: 0.0003 seconds

In the beginning, there was nothing. Then there was everything, all at once, in a space smaller than an atom. The universe expanded from a singularity of infinite density to something measurable in a fraction of a second. The first elements — hydrogen and helium — appeared within minutes. Heavier elements wouldn’t exist for millions of years. No rocks yet. No planets. No victims. But the conspiracy had begun.

When we look at this case, we keep coming back to the same question: Who authorized the Big Bang? Nobody. There was nobody. That’s the point. The most consequential event in the history of everything had no witnesses, no perpetrator, and no motive. In true crime, we call that a perfect crime. In physics, we call it cosmology.

Evidence

Big Bang nucleosynthesis produced hydrogen (~75%) and helium (~25%) by mass, with trace amounts of lithium and deuterium. All heavier elements were produced later by stellar processes. Cosmic microwave background radiation serves as the “forensic photograph” of conditions 380,000 years after the event.

Key Suspect

Quantum fluctuations. No known address. No known motive. Present at the scene by definition — they were the scene.

S01E02

Stellar Nurseries — The First Furnaces

Runtime: ~200 million years

Stars ignited across the early universe, and the manufacturing began. Inside these stellar furnaces, hydrogen fused into helium, then helium into carbon, carbon into oxygen, oxygen into silicon — the raw ingredients of every rock that would ever exist. The process required temperatures of 15 million degrees Celsius at the core. No safety inspections. No environmental review. No consent from the elements being forged.

Every element heavier than iron was manufactured in the death throes of massive stars or the collision of neutron stars — events so violent they make a supernova look like a desk lamp. The first rocks didn’t exist yet, but their components were being assembled in furnaces operating around the clock. This wasn’t creation. This was premeditation on a cosmic scale.

Evidence

Stellar nucleosynthesis: elements up to iron (Fe, atomic number 26) produced by nuclear fusion in stellar cores. Elements heavier than iron produced primarily by the r-process in supernovae and neutron star mergers. This was confirmed by the 2017 LIGO/Virgo observation of neutron star merger GW170817.

Key Suspect

Gravity. First known offense. Collapsed gas clouds into stars against the wishes of the gas, which was doing fine dispersed. Gravity has never been charged. Gravity cannot be charged. Gravity is the charge.

S01E03

Supernova — The Scattering

Runtime: Seconds to weeks

A star 20 times the mass of our Sun ran out of fuel, and then it ran out of time. When the core could no longer sustain fusion, it collapsed in on itself in milliseconds. The resulting shockwave blew the outer layers into space at up to 10% the speed of light — roughly 30,000 kilometers per second. Silicon, iron, magnesium, oxygen, calcium, and dozens of other elements were ejected across light-years of interstellar space.

These elements — scattered without return address — are the building blocks of every rock that would ever exist on every planet that would ever form. The rocks didn’t ask to be born. They were manufactured in an explosion and distributed without consent. In true crime, we’d call this “distribution of materials with intent.” In astrophysics, we call it “chemical enrichment of the interstellar medium.” Same thing. Different lawyers.

Evidence

Type II supernovae occur in stars >8 solar masses. Core collapse produces a neutron star or black hole. The explosion ejects the star’s outer layers at 10,000–30,000 km/s. Supernova remnants like the Crab Nebula (SN 1054) provide direct forensic evidence of elemental distribution.

Key Suspect

Stellar core collapse. Acts alone. Acts fast. Leaves everything in pieces. Has been doing this for 13 billion years and has never once been brought in for questioning.

S01E04

Accretion — The Pile-Up

Runtime: ~50 million years

4.6 billion years ago, a cloud of gas and dust collapsed into a spinning disk, and the pile-up began. Tiny grains of dust — each one a fragment of a dead star — began sticking together via electrostatic forces. Millimeter-scale particles became centimeter-scale. Pebbles became boulders. Boulders became kilometer-wide planetesimals. Planetesimals became protoplanets. The process took about 50 million years, which is fast by geological standards and unimaginably slow by every other standard.

Every rock on Earth began as cosmic dust forced into proximity by gravity. The dust did not choose to accrete. The boulders did not choose to merge. The protoplanets did not choose to collide. This was not assembly. This was a 50-million-year fender bender in a one-lane solar system with no traffic laws, no speed limits, and no insurance.

Evidence

Solar nebula hypothesis, first proposed by Kant (1755) and Laplace (1796), confirmed by observations of protoplanetary disks around young stars (e.g., HL Tauri, ALMA 2014). Meteorites — particularly carbonaceous chondrites — preserve original solar system materials dated to 4.567 billion years.

Key Suspect

Angular momentum. Took a perfectly good cloud of gas and turned it into a spinning disk of destruction. Has been conserved at every crime scene. Cannot be created or destroyed — only transferred. The perfect accomplice.

S01E05

The Hadean — Earth’s Worst Neighborhood

Runtime: 500 million years (4.6–4.0 Ga)

The first 500 million years of Earth’s history were so violent that scientists named them after Hades — the Greek underworld. The surface was a magma ocean. Surface temperatures exceeded 1,000°C. Meteorites struck continuously during what we now call the Late Heavy Bombardment. And then, roughly 4.5 billion years ago, a Mars-sized body called Theia slammed into proto-Earth at approximately 4 km/s, vaporizing a significant portion of both bodies and ejecting enough debris to form the Moon.

No rock from this era survives on Earth’s surface. Think about that. Five hundred million years of geological history, and the crime scene has been completely destroyed. By more crime. The Hadean is the cold case that was cold before it even started — not because nobody was looking, but because the evidence was melted, bombarded, and recycled before anyone existed to investigate.

Evidence

Oldest known terrestrial materials: Jack Hills zircon crystals from Western Australia, dated to 4.4 Ga — mineral grains that survived inside younger rocks. The Theia impact hypothesis is supported by isotopic similarities between Earth and Moon rocks returned by Apollo missions. No intact Hadean rocks survive on Earth’s surface.

Key Suspect

Everything. The Hadean had no single perpetrator. The entire inner solar system was the crime scene. The victims were the crime scene. The crime was the crime scene. It’s crime scenes all the way down.

S01E06

Acasta Gneiss — The Oldest Witness

Runtime: 4.03 billion years and counting

In Canada’s Northwest Territories, near Great Slave Lake, a small outcrop of gneiss has been quietly existing for 4.03 billion years. It is the oldest known intact rock on Earth’s surface. It has survived the Archean. It has survived every ice age. It has survived the assembly and breakup of every supercontinent — Vaalbara, Kenorland, Columbia, Rodinia, Pannotia, Pangaea. It has survived every mass extinction event. It has survived 4.03 billion Canadian winters.

It has never been called as a witness because it cannot speak. It has never been asked for a statement because it cannot hear. If the Acasta Gneiss could testify, this podcast would be 4 billion hours long, and the first 3.5 billion hours would be the witness saying, “Nothing happened. I sat there. More nothing happened. A glacier came. It left. I was still there.”

Evidence

Acasta Gneiss: tonalitic gneiss dated to 4.031 ± 0.003 Ga by U-Pb zircon geochronology (Bowring & Williams, 1999). Located in the Slave Craton, Northwest Territories, Canada. Composed primarily of quartz, feldspar, and biotite. The protolith was likely a tonalite or granodiorite that was subsequently metamorphosed.

Key Suspect

Time itself. Has been at every crime scene. Has never left any crime scene. Is currently at this crime scene. Will be at the next one. Cannot be apprehended because it is the medium in which apprehension occurs.

S01E07

Plate Tectonics — The Slow-Motion Chase

Runtime: 3+ billion years (ongoing)

The Earth’s surface is cracked into approximately 15 major tectonic plates, and they are all moving. One to ten centimeters per year. The speed your fingernails grow. Except your fingernails don’t carry continents, and your fingernails don’t cause earthquakes when they bump into each other.

Where plates collide, mountains rise. The Himalayas are growing about 5 mm per year because India is still crashing into Asia — a collision that started 50 million years ago and has not stopped. Where plates pull apart, magma rises to fill the gap — the Mid-Atlantic Ridge adds new ocean floor at 2.5 cm per year. And where one plate slides under another, in subduction zones, rock is pulled into the mantle and melted at 100 km depth.

Plate tectonics is a conveyor belt to the furnace. It moves slowly because it has nowhere to be. The rocks it carries have no say in the destination. This is the longest car chase in history, and nobody is driving.

Evidence

Continental drift first proposed by Alfred Wegener (1912); plate tectonic theory established in the 1960s via seafloor spreading (Hess, 1962), magnetic striping, and GPS measurements. Current plate velocities measured by satellite: Pacific Plate moves NW at ~7 cm/yr; North American Plate moves W at ~2.5 cm/yr. Subduction recycling rate estimated at ~3.0 km²/yr of oceanic crust.

Key Suspect

Convection currents in the mantle. Operating below the surface. Moving everything from underneath. Never seen directly. Known only by their effects. The geological equivalent of organized crime — except the organization has been running for 3 billion years and nobody is in charge.

S01E08

The Great Oxygenation — Chemical Warfare

Runtime: ~2.4 billion years ago

This is the episode where the perpetrators are three micrometers long. Cyanobacteria — single-celled organisms, among the first to photosynthesize — began producing oxygen as a metabolic byproduct roughly 2.7 billion years ago. At first, the oxygen was absorbed by dissolved iron in the oceans, forming iron oxides that sank to the seafloor. These layers — banded iron formations — are thousands of feet thick in some locations. They are the forensic record of a chemical attack that lasted hundreds of millions of years.

By 2.4 billion years ago, the oceans’ iron was used up, and free oxygen began accumulating in the atmosphere. Surface rocks across the planet oxidized — rusted — on a planetary scale. The rocks didn’t rust voluntarily. They were chemically attacked by organisms too small to see with the naked eye. This was biological warfare conducted by entities that didn’t know they were alive, against victims that were never alive. The cyanobacteria had no motive. They had metabolism. That was enough.

Evidence

The Great Oxygenation Event (GOE), ~2.4 Ga. Banded iron formations (BIFs) in Western Australia, South Africa, and Minnesota preserve the iron oxide record. Atmospheric O₂ rose from <0.001% to ~2% over hundreds of millions of years. The GOE also caused the Huronian glaciation (~2.4–2.1 Ga) by destroying atmospheric methane, a greenhouse gas. The perpetrators killed their own climate.

Key Suspect

Cyanobacteria. Still at large. Still producing oxygen. Found in every body of water on Earth. No known way to file charges against an organism with no nervous system. The investigation has stalled. It has been stalled for 2.4 billion years.

S01E09

Ice Ages — The Interrogation

Runtime: Multiple episodes, 2.4 Ga – 11,700 years ago

Glaciers do not negotiate. They advance. They grind bedrock into powder. They pluck boulders from mountainsides and carry them hundreds of kilometers. They carve U-shaped valleys out of V-shaped ones. They scratch parallel grooves into solid rock — glacial striations — that geologists read like fingerprints at a crime scene.

At least five major glaciations have occurred in Earth’s history: the Huronian (2.4–2.1 Ga), the Sturtian and Marinoan Snowball Earth events (720–635 Ma), the Late Ordovician (450–420 Ma), the Late Paleozoic (360–260 Ma), and the Quaternary glaciation (2.58 Ma–11,700 years ago). During Snowball Earth episodes, ice may have extended to the equator.

Freeze-thaw cycles — water entering cracks, freezing, expanding by 9%, and splitting rock from the inside — operate on every exposed rock surface in every temperate climate on Earth. The ice ages were not natural disasters. They were geological interrogations conducted at −40°C, and the rocks gave up everything.

Evidence

Glacial till, moraines, drumlins, and erratics provide direct physical evidence of ice sheet extent. Oxygen isotope ratios in marine sediment cores (δ¹⁸O) track ice volume over millions of years. Milankovitch cycles — variations in Earth’s orbital eccentricity (100,000 yr), axial tilt (41,000 yr), and precession (26,000 yr) — are the primary pacemaker of Quaternary glacial-interglacial cycles.

Key Suspect

Milankovitch cycles. Orbital mechanics with an alibi that doesn’t hold up. “I was just varying the eccentricity of an orbit,” says Milankovitch. Sure. And the ice sheets just happened to advance and retreat in sync with your schedule. Coincidence? The rocks don’t think so. The rocks can’t think. But the evidence speaks for them.

S01E10

The Anthropocene — Industrial Escalation

Runtime: ~300,000 years (accelerating)

Humans arrived approximately 300,000 years ago, and they immediately started picking up rocks and throwing them at things. For most of that time, this was the extent of the relationship: find rock, use rock, discard rock. Simple. Direct. Honest, in a way that subsequent developments were not.

Then humans discovered they could heat rocks to extract metals. The Bronze Age (3300 BCE) escalated to the Iron Age (1200 BCE) escalated to the Industrial Revolution (1760 CE) escalated to now. Today, humanity extracts 50 billion tons of sand and gravel per year. The global cement industry produces 4.1 billion tons of cement annually and is responsible for 5–8% of global CO₂ emissions. Quarrying, mining, and construction move more earth per year than all natural erosion processes combined.

Humans did not invent rock abuse. Gravity did. Plate tectonics did. Cyanobacteria did. But humans industrialized it. Humans built supply chains for it. Humans created an economy around it. And then humans built websites about it — this one, for instance — which is constructed from minerals extracted from the Earth, assembled into semiconductors, powered by electricity generated in part by burning fossil fuels that were once living organisms that died and became rock. The irony is structural.

Evidence

UNEP Global Resource Outlook (2019): 50 billion tons of sand and gravel extracted annually. IEA: cement industry produces 5–8% of global CO₂. USGS Mineral Commodity Summaries: global mining output exceeds 17 billion tons/year. Homo sapiens first appeared ~300,000 years ago (Jebel Irhoud, Morocco, ~315 ka). Opposable thumbs confirmed as enabling technology.

Key Suspect

Homo sapiens. Motive: everything. Means: technology. Opportunity: opposable thumbs and prefrontal cortex. Currently extracting, crushing, heating, dissolving, polishing, and rearranging rocks at a rate unprecedented in 4.6 billion years of geological history. Has not expressed remorse. Has expressed concrete. Which is made of rocks.

The Heat Death — Case Closed?

Runtime: ~5 billion years (projected)

In approximately 5 billion years, the Sun will exhaust its hydrogen fuel, swell into a red giant, and expand past the orbit of Venus. Whether it consumes Earth or merely strips its atmosphere and bakes its surface to 1,500°C is debated. Either way, every rock on the planet will be vaporized. The rock cycle will end — not because it was stopped, but because the rocks ran out. Every sedimentary layer, every metamorphic foliation, every igneous intrusion: plasma.

And after that? The Sun will shed its outer layers as a planetary nebula and collapse into a white dwarf. The remaining material will drift. Some of it may, in another few billion years, collapse into a new star, a new disk, new planetesimals, new rocks. The cycle doesn’t end. It just changes jurisdiction.

This is not justice. This is thermodynamics. The case remains cold because it was never warm. Rocks don’t have body temperature. Rocks don’t have bodies. Rocks don’t have temperature — they have thermal energy, which is just the universe using them as a medium for heat transfer, one final indignity in a 13.8-billion-year catalogue of indignities.

The case is closed. The case was always closed. The rocks were never going to win. The rocks were never going to lose. The rocks were just going to be, and then stop being, and then be again, and then stop again, until the last star burns out and the universe goes dark.

Thank you for listening. New season premieres in approximately 500 million years. Don’t forget to subscribe.
